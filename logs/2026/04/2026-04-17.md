# Captain's Log — 2026-04-17
Mostly UI polish day on a private project — the kind of work that doesn't feel glamorous but makes or breaks how users perceive a product.

The bulk of the effort went into the account and dashboard surfaces. Refined the personal info layout, which touched seven files — more than you'd expect for a "layout tweak," but that usually means untangling some coupling between display logic and data structure that was never quite right to begin with. Alongside that, fixed the empty state copy on the account dashboard. Small change, two files, but empty states are one of those places where bad copy genuinely erodes trust. If there's nothing to show, you still have to say something useful.

The more spec-driven work was aligning the ProfileCard dashboard tile with a design ticket. That kind of work is less about judgment and more about precision — making sure spacing, hierarchy, and content match what was agreed on. Not the most creatively satisfying work, but necessary for maintaining coherence across a component library.

The login redirect fix is the one I'm most glad to have shipped. Redirect handling after authentication is one of those deceptively tricky areas — you're dealing with state that exists across a navigation boundary, and there are usually at least a few edge cases around where the user was trying to go before they hit the auth wall. Five files changed suggests it wasn't a one-liner; probably touched the auth flow, some routing logic, and maybe session or storage handling.

Solid, focused day. Nothing revolutionary, but the kind of work that keeps a product from feeling rough around the edges.
