# Captain's Log — 2026-04-22
Three separate projects touched today, which always makes for a fragmented kind of day — context-switching taxes you in ways that don't show up in the commit count.

The most interesting work was in private-project-1: a new `EventCardCompact` component paired with a transparent variant for an existing `Tag` component. Compact card variants are one of those things that seem simple until you're actually doing them — you're not just shrinking the layout, you're making real decisions about what information survives the cut. What context can a user lose and still understand what they're looking at? Getting that balance right while keeping the component composable enough to be useful in different contexts takes some care. The transparent `Tag` variant is a small thing, but these kinds of presentational additions usually signal that designs are evolving and the existing variants aren't covering all the real use cases anymore. Six files changed for two additions is reasonable — probably the component itself, a story or test, some style definitions, and a few integration points.

The other two projects were largely deployment mechanics: a "create contribution flow" feature propagated across both private-project-2 and private-project-3, moving from develop through staging and out to production. Four files changed in private-project-3 for that merge suggests the feature had already done most of its landing earlier — this was the tail end of a longer integration arc. The rapid open-merge-push cadence on private-project-2 across two separate PRs (the contribution flow and a staging sync) tells the familiar story of keeping environments aligned after a feature ships.

Honestly, days like this are more operational than creative. The real work on the contribution flow happened earlier; today was about getting it across the finish line safely. The UI component work was the one place where there was genuine design thinking involved, even if the surface area was modest.
