# Captain's Log — 2026-02-17
Small day — one commit, but it touched 12 files, which tells the real story. The work was all copy and UX text refinement across the contribution and contributor flows in a private project I've been building out. This kind of task sounds minor until you're staring at a dozen screens trying to make sure the language is consistent, appropriately formal or informal depending on context, and actually guides the user rather than just labeling things.

Text work in UI flows is deceptively fiddly. You're not just wordsmithing in isolation — you're thinking about how a label reads mid-flow when the user is already three steps in, whether a call-to-action verb matches the mental model of what they're about to do, and whether the tone shifts awkwardly between screens. Touching 12 files for what's essentially copy changes usually means those strings are spread across components rather than centralized, which is a reminder that i18n or at least a shared copy layer would pay dividends here eventually.

Nothing architecturally interesting today, but this kind of polish pass is genuinely important. Rough edges in onboarding flows are where you lose people, and contributor flows specifically need to feel trustworthy and clear — users making a decision to contribute something are in a different headspace than passive browsers. Getting the language right matters.
