Mostly navigation and routing work today — the kind of unglamorous-but-necessary stuff that quietly breaks user flows when it goes wrong.

The bulk of the commits landed in a private project where I was touching the navbar configuration. Centralizing URL constants is one of those decisions that pays dividends over time — when a route needs to change, you touch one place instead of hunting down hardcoded strings scattered across the codebase. Two files changed, which suggests this was a targeted, intentional update rather than a sprawling refactor.

Not a heavy day, but routing and navigation correctness matters more than the effort involved would suggest. A broken link or a missing route is often the first thing a user encounters, and it sets the tone for everything that follows.
