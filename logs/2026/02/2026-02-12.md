# Captain's Log — 2026-02-12
Relatively light day split between two private projects, but the work was meaningful.

Most of the morning went toward a couple of small but user-facing changes in one project. Adding solicitors to an invoice form sounds mundane, but that kind of domain-specific data entry work matters — the LAH invoice form presumably drives real billing or legal workflows, and getting the right entities listed is the difference between the form being usable and not. The navbar change — stripping years from event labels — is the kind of UX cleanup that's easy to overlook but quietly improves things. Year-scoped navigation labels tend to age poorly and clutter the UI; removing them suggests the events are either recurring or better surfaced through other filtering mechanisms. One file each, clean and focused.

The more interesting commit was in the second project: a new press room endpoint. The implementation pulls data via a REST API fetch and then rewrites CDN URLs before serving the response. That URL rewriting step is worth calling out — it's the kind of middleware concern that's easy to skip initially and painful to retrofit later. Whether it's normalizing asset domains, enforcing HTTPS, or routing through a cache layer, having that transformation happen server-side keeps the client clean and centralizes control over where media actually comes from. Five files changed for a new endpoint is a reasonable footprint — probably a route definition, a service or fetch utility, some URL rewriting logic, and a test or two.

Nothing earth-shattering today, but the press room work has good bones.
