# Captain's Log — 2026-02-10
Three commits today, split across two private projects — not a heavy day, but meaningful touches on both.

Most of the morning went toward private-project-1. Updating submodules to latest dev is one of those small but necessary bits of hygiene that quietly prevents drift from becoming a real problem later. When you're tracking a fast-moving dependency, letting submodule pointers stale out even a few weeks can mean reconciling a wall of changes instead of a trickle. Keeping that pointer current is cheap; catching up later isn't.

The more interesting addition to that same project was dropping in a CLAUDE.md. If you haven't seen this pattern yet, it's a conventions file specifically aimed at giving Claude (Anthropic's AI assistant) context about a codebase — how it's structured, what style it follows, what footguns to avoid. It's an emerging practice, somewhere between a CONTRIBUTING.md and a system prompt baked into the repo itself. Adding one now, early in the project's life, is the right call. It's much easier to write this kind of document when the decisions are fresh than to reverse-engineer your own reasoning six months later.

The other commit was in private-project-2 and tackled a real authentication edge case: falling back to Application Default Credentials when the expected Firebase Admin environment variables aren't set. This is a pattern that comes up constantly in GCP-adjacent stacks — you want explicit credentials in production but you don't want local development or CI to choke just because someone hasn't configured every env var. The fix probably involved checking for the presence of those variables before initializing the Admin SDK with them, and gracefully deferring to ADC otherwise. Clean, practical, and the kind of thing that makes onboarding new contributors significantly less painful.

Low commit count, but nothing throwaway here.
