# Captain's Log — 2026-02-18
Light day on the infrastructure side. All three commits touched the same private project, and the work was squarely in the repo-management category rather than feature development.

The most interesting bit was removing `.gitmodules` outright. That file is the manifest for Git submodules, so its removal signals either a full abandonment of the submodule pattern or a clean slate before restructuring how the backend and frontend are linked. Given that the very next commit manually updates those submodule references to their latest dev branches, I suspect this was a deliberate reset — strip the old config, recommit the pointers fresh. Submodule management is one of those things that silently accumulates drift over time, especially when multiple people are pushing to different branches. A clean break is sometimes the right call, even if it creates a moment of friction.

The contributor guidelines doc rounds out the day. Writing that kind of documentation isn't glamorous, but it pays real dividends as a project grows. Getting conventions, branch strategies, and review expectations written down early means you spend less time in Slack threads explaining the same things over and over. Better to do it now than after you've already onboarded three people the wrong way.

Short day, but the kind of housekeeping that quietly keeps a project healthy.
