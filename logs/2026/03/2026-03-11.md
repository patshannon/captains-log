# Captain's Log — 2026-03-11
Heavy day on the frontend — three PRs merged, 9 commits across two repos, and a real sense of forward momentum on what's shaping up to be a content-rich web application.

The bulk of the work landed in private-project-1, where I shipped three feature branches into develop in quick succession. The home page content section (the biggest of the three by file count) involved 30 files and pulled in `@tailwindcss/typography` to give rich text content the formatting treatment it deserves — that plugin is one of those quiet quality-of-life additions that pays dividends whenever you're rendering Sanity portable text or markdown. I also added alt text support to the Hero component and its corresponding Sanity schema, which is the kind of accessibility detail that's easy to defer and harder to retrofit later. Good to get it baked in now.

The footer component was more involved than footers usually are. Accordion behavior, link groups, and social media integration means it's a real layout component with interactive state, not just a static block at the bottom of the page. Accordion components in particular always surface testing edge cases — I had to fix a visibility check in the `AccordionItem` tests to correctly assert on hidden content, which is a subtle thing to get right depending on how you're toggling display versus visibility versus aria attributes. Worth taking the time to get the test semantics correct rather than papering over it.

The account dashboard PR was the most feature-dense: dashboard cards, upcoming events, and a newsletter signup — essentially the skeleton of a logged-in user experience. Lots of surface area in 48 files, and the kind of work where you have to be disciplined about component boundaries early or pay for it later with tangled state.

On the tooling side, I updated the captains-log scraper to capture push and pull request events in addition to commits, then resynced the historical logs. The data model is more complete now — commits alone miss too much of the workflow story.
