# Captain's Log — 2026-03-16
Light day on the surface, but the commit itself was meaningful work. Spent most of my focus on a refactor inside a private project — pulling out a shared SSO handler hook that had been living too close to a specific provider's implementation. The Google SSO feature branch made it obvious the pattern was going to repeat: you can't just copy-paste auth flow logic every time you add a new identity provider without things getting messy fast.

The extract was worth doing properly. Eleven files touched, which reflects how embedded the original logic had become — not a clean lift, more of a careful untangling. The payoff is that adding Facebook as a provider on top of it was straightforward once the abstraction was solid. That's the real test of a good hook design: the second consumer should feel easy, not like you're forcing a square peg.

SSO integrations have a way of looking simple until you're deep in token handling, redirect flows, and provider-specific quirks. Getting the shared layer right now, while there are only two providers, is the kind of debt prevention that pays off quietly — you just never have the painful rewrite later.
